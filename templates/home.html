Visualizing Connections Between Class and Air Quality

While it is widely known that our world’s path towards climate catastrophe is a threat to all life on earth, the communities that are most vulnerable to these changes are often overlooked. That is why we have focused our tool, PollutionPlot, to draw attention to these inequities. Our team has created an interactive tool which allows you to see the air quality and median incomes placed side by side for most counties of a given state.

HOW TO READ THIS GRAPH

What is AQI?

AQI is a measure from 0 - 500 that describes daily air quality. Lower AQI values occur on days with good air quality, and higher AQI values occur on days with bad air quality.

90th Percentile AQI

90th percentile AQI is effectively a measure of "how bad it gets" with regards to air quality in a given county. It is the 90th percentile value for AQI recorded in a county in 2019. We feel this is the best metric to lend insight into the correlation between class and air quality as it puts a quantitative value on the pollution residents face without bias towards extreme weather events.
